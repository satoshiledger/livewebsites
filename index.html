LLC, Corporation, or S-Corp?

Partnership vs. S-Corp Election

In Puerto Rico, an LLC is a legal structure, but how it is taxed makes the difference. Choosing LLC/Partnership: Ideal for flexibility where all income flows to your personal return. Choosing S-Corp: Beneficial when profits exceed $60k-$100k, allowing you to split salary from distributions to save on self-employment taxes.

  • LLCs offer simpler compliance for new ventures.
  • S-Corps require "Reasonable Salary" and payroll.
  • Corporations face double taxation unless exempt.

Top 20 Act 60 & Compliance FAQs

1. Residency & Individual Investor

What is the 183-day presence test? ▼

To be a bona fide resident, you must be in PR for 183+ days, or meet multi-year harbors (549 days over 3 years).

What are the annual donation and property rules? ▼

Decree holders must donate $10,000 annually to approved non-profits and purchase a home in PR within 2 years of moving.

Are crypto capital gains 100% exempt? ▼

Gains accrued *after* you become a PR resident are 100% exempt. Pre-residency gains are still taxable by the IRS.

What is the "Closer Connection" test? ▼

You must prove your primary social, family, and financial ties are in PR, not the mainland.

How are dividends and interest from U.S. banks taxed? ▼

PR-sourced interest is exempt; however, interest from U.S. sources may still be subject to federal income tax.

2. Export Services & PyMEs

What is the 4% Export Services rate? ▼

Eligible businesses exporting services (consulting, software) to clients outside PR pay only a 4% corporate tax rate.

Who is a "Young Entrepreneur" (Act 60 Chapter 3)? ▼

Entrepreneurs aged 16–35 qualify for 100% tax exemption on the first $500k of net income for 3 years.

What is a "New PyME" 2% tax rate? ▼

Special incentives allow for a 2% corporate tax rate for small and medium enterprises during initial operating years.

Are local PR sales included in the 4% rate? ▼

No. The 4% rate applies strictly to services exported. Local sales are taxed at standard corporate rates.

What is the tax rate for Qualified Physicians? ▼

Qualifying specialists and physicians enjoy a flat 4% income tax rate on professional services.

3. Entity Setup & Compliance

LLC vs. Corporation: Which is better? ▼

LLCs offer pass-through flexibility and fewer corporate formalities compared to traditional Corporations.

What is SURI and why is it mandatory? ▼

SURI is the digital platform for Hacienda. It is where you register your Merchant's Number and file monthly IVU.

What is a Municipal Patent? ▼

A "Volume of Business" tax paid to the municipality where you operate, due annually every April.

How does FICA work for PR employees? ▼

Social Security and Medicare (FICA) are identical to the US mainland: 15.3% total split between employer/employee.
