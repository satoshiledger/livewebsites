LLC Pass-Through vs. S-Corp Election

The Strategy

Save ~$7,650 annually on FICA by electing S-Corp status.

In an LLC, all $100k of net profit is subject to 15.3% Self-Employment tax. By electing S-Corp status and paying a $50k "reasonable salary," you shield the other $50k from FICA.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies as a "Young Entrepreneur"? ▼

Residents aged 16-35 starting a new business qualify for 100% exemption on the first $500k of net income for 3 years.

When is a "Reasonable Salary" required? ▼

If you elect S-Corp status to save on FICA, you must pay yourself a salary consistent with industry standards to avoid IRS audits.

Do I need to pay IVU on exported services? ▼

Services exported outside PR are typically 0% IVU rated, but monthly SURI returns are still mandatory.
