What is the Optional Tax Method?

Puerto Rico's Optional Tax Method allows professionals to pay a flat 6-20% tax on gross income instead of the standard 0-33% graduated rates on net income. Available to self-employed individuals, corporations, and LLCs providing professional services.

Optional Tax Rate Brackets:

Gross income up to $100,000 6%
$100,001 - $250,000 10%
$250,001 - $500,000 15%
Over $500,000 20%

Key Requirement:

At least 80% of your income must come from providing services.

For self-employed professionals in Puerto Rico, one of the most innovative tax strategies available under the island's tax code is the Optional Tax Method. Introduced through the Puerto Rico Tax Reform of 2018 (Act 257-2018), this alternative taxation system allows eligible service providers to pay a flat percentage on their gross income rather than navigating the complexity of the standard graduated tax structure that can reach 33% on net income.

For lawyers, engineers, accountants, consultants, and other professional service providers, this option can result in substantial tax savings while dramatically simplifying compliance requirements. Learn more about Puerto Rico's tax incentives on our Act 60 Mastery page.

Puerto Rico Standard Tax Rates (0%-33%)

Before exploring the Optional Tax Method, it's essential to understand the traditional tax structure that Puerto Rico residents face. Puerto Rico operates its own independent tax system, separate from the United States federal income tax structure. The island employs a graduated income tax system with rates that increase as income rises.

Individual Income Tax Brackets:

Income up to $9,000 0%
$9,001 - $25,000 7%
$25,001 - $41,500 14%
$41,501 - $61,500 25%
Over $61,500 33%

Additionally, self-employed individuals must navigate the Alternative Basic Tax system, which adds another layer of complexity. For individuals with net taxable income exceeding $500,000, an additional gradual adjustment tax of 5% applies. This means that high-earning professionals can face effective tax rates well above the stated 33% maximum when all adjustments and calculations are considered.

Optional Tax Method: How It Works

The Optional Tax Method represents a fundamental shift in how Puerto Rico taxes professional service providers. Introduced through Act 257-2018, this system allows self-employed individuals AND corporations (including LLCs taxed as corporations) whose income derives substantially from rendering services to elect to pay a fixed tax rate on gross income instead of the traditional income tax imposed on net income.

Entity Types Eligible:

Self-employed individuals (sole proprietors)
Corporations providing services
LLCs taxed as corporations

Progressive Rate Structure on Gross Income:

6%
On gross income up to $100,000
10%
On gross income $100,001-$250,000
15%
On gross income $250,001-$500,000
20%
On gross income over $500,000

Important Note:

These rates apply to the entire gross income, not just the amount within each bracket, making calculations remarkably straightforward compared to the graduated system.

Eligibility: 80% Service Income Requirement

To qualify for the Optional Tax Method, professionals must meet specific criteria established by the Puerto Rico Treasury Department. The most critical requirement is the 80% threshold.

The 80% Rule:

At least 80% of total gross income during the taxable year must be derived from the provision of services. This threshold ensures that the benefit is reserved for true service professionals rather than businesses that combine service delivery with product sales or other revenue streams.

Additional Requirements:

Informative Return

All gross income must be included in an informative return and subject to withholding at source or estimated tax payments

No Balance Owed

There must be no balance owed with the income tax return. The entire tax liability must be covered through withholdings or estimated payments throughout the year

Service Income Only

Non-service income cannot exceed 20% of total gross income to maintain qualification

The Trade-Off: No Deductions Allowed

The Optional Tax Method's most significant consideration is the elimination of deductions. Under the standard tax system, self-employed professionals can deduct ordinary and necessary business expenses such as:

Employee wages
Rent & utilities
Advertising
Professional services
Travel expenses
Equipment costs

However, when electing the Optional Tax Method, professionals cannot claim these deductions. This trade-off represents the core decision point for professionals considering the Optional Tax Method.

Who Benefits Most:

Those with minimal business expenses and high gross-to-net income ratios benefit substantially from the simplified calculation. Conversely, professionals with significant deductible expenses may find that the standard system results in lower overall tax liability.

Should You Choose the Optional Tax Method?

YES if you have:

  • Low business expenses (< 20% of revenue)
  • High gross-to-net income ratio
  • Simple business structure
  • Want administrative simplicity
  • Solo practitioner or small firm

NO if you have:

  • High deductible expenses (> 30% of revenue)
  • Significant capital investments planned
  • Complex corporate structure
  • Multiple revenue streams
  • Large employee payroll

How to Elect the Optional Tax Method

The procedural requirements are straightforward but must be followed precisely. The Puerto Rico Treasury Department issued Internal Revenue Circular Letter No. 19-02 in February 2019, establishing the process.

1

Request Withholding Waiver

For income up to $100,000, request a partial waiver so that applicable withholding is reduced to 6% instead of the ordinary 10% rate

2

File Sworn Statement

Deliver a sworn statement to each payor, which constitutes an election to be subject to the optional tax for that taxable year

3

Make Estimated Payments

Individuals: Final payment by January 15 of following year. Corporations: 15th day of final month of fiscal year

4

File Annual Return

Ensure all taxes are paid with no balance owed when filing the return

Annual Flexibility:

The Optional Tax Method election is made annually when filing the income tax return, providing flexibility to switch between systems as circumstances change.

Conclusion: Innovation Meets Simplicity

The Optional Tax Method represents an innovative approach to taxation that recognizes the unique characteristics of professional service businesses. By offering a simplified flat-rate structure applied to gross income, Puerto Rico has created a system that reduces compliance burden while maintaining competitive effective tax rates for many professionals.

For lawyers, engineers, accountants, consultants, and other service providers operating in Puerto Rico, the decision between the Optional Tax Method and the standard graduated system requires careful analysis of individual circumstances. Those with high net-to-gross income ratios, minimal deductible expenses, and a preference for administrative simplicity will likely find the Optional Tax Method delivers both financial and operational advantages.

The flexibility to elect annually between the two systems provides professionals with the ability to optimize their tax position as circumstances evolve. This flexibility, combined with the substantial potential tax savings and administrative simplification, makes the Optional Tax Method one of the most valuable provisions in Puerto Rico's tax code for self-employed service professionals.
