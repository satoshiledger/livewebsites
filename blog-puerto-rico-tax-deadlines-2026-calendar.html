Every Puerto Rico Tax Deadline in 2026: The Complete Filing Calendar for Businesses Cada Fecha Límite de Impuestos de Puerto Rico en 2026: Calendario Completo para Negocios

Share this article:Compartir este articulo:

Operating a business in Puerto Rico means navigating a dual filing system — Hacienda (Puerto Rico Treasury), the IRS, DDEC, CRIM, and the Department of State all have separate deadlines, separate portals, and separate penalties for late compliance. Miss one, and you risk fines, interest, or even losing your Act 60 decree.

This calendar consolidates every major tax deadline for calendar-year businesses and Act 60 decree holders operating in Puerto Rico in 2026. Bookmark it, share it with your CPA, and set your reminders early — Hacienda does not offer extensions for informative returns, and DDEC started automatically issuing fines for late annual report filings in January 2026 (Tax Notes, Oct. 2025; Hacienda Publication 25-03).

Important Notes

All dates below assume a calendar-year taxpayer (Jan. 1 – Dec. 31). Fiscal-year entities should adjust dates accordingly — the general rule is the 15th day of the Nth month after close of fiscal year. When a deadline falls on a weekend or PR/federal holiday, it shifts to the next business day. All monetary returns and estimated payments must be filed electronically through SURI (Sabalier Law, Jan. 2025; Kreston PR, Jan. 2025).

Month-by-Month Calendar: January – June 2026

January 2026

Jan 15

Q4 2025 Estimated Income Tax (Individual) — Final quarterly estimated payment for tax year 2025. File and pay electronically through SURI (CFS PR; Sabalier Law).

Jan 15

DDEC Automatic Fines Begin — Starting Jan. 15, 2026, DDEC's OIN automatically issues deficiency notices and fines ($1,000+) for decree holders who failed to file their 2024 annual exempt business report (IANE) by Nov. 15, 2025 (Tax Notes, Oct. 2025; News Is My Business, Oct. 2025).

Jan 20

IVU Monthly Return — December 2025 sales and use tax due. File through SURI (DAVO; Grant Thornton PR).

Jan 31

Payee Copies of 480 Forms — Provide copies of Form 480.6A, 480.6B, 480.6SP, 480.7A, 480.7D, and other informative returns to recipients (payees, contractors, landlords). This is the deadline for recipient delivery, not Hacienda filing (Tax1099, Oct. 2025; Bookkeeping Services, Mar. 2025).

Jan 31

W-2PR to Employees — Provide 2025 Form 499R-2/W-2PR to all employees.

February 2026

Feb 15

Personal Property Estimated Tax (Q3) — Third quarterly installment for tax year 2025 personal property tax. Pay through CRIM portal (Kreston PR; CPA Puerto Rico).

Feb 20

IVU Monthly Return — January 2026 IVU due via SURI.

Feb 28

Informative Returns Filed with Hacienda — Most 480 Series forms due to Hacienda: Forms 480.6A, 480.6B, 480.6SP (services rendered), 480.7, 480.7C, and corresponding summary Form 480.5. File electronically through SURI. Note: Hacienda does not grant extensions for informative returns (Hacienda Pub. 25-03; CPA Puerto Rico; Tax1099).

March 2026

Mar 20

IVU Monthly Return — February 2026 IVU due via SURI.

Mar 31

Electronic Filing Deadline for 480 Forms — Final deadline for electronic filing of informative returns with Hacienda via SURI (Bookkeeping Services, Mar. 2025; Tax1099).

April 2026 — Major Filing Month

Apr 15

PR Individual Income Tax Return (Form 482) — File 2025 return and pay any balance due. Report worldwide income; claim Act 60 exemptions in Schedule B3. Auto 6-month extension available (to Oct. 15) but does not extend payment deadline (LegalClarity, Dec. 2025; Bookkeeping Services, Jul. 2025).

Apr 15

PR Corporate Income Tax Return (Form 480.20) — File 2025 corporate return for calendar-year corporations. Due 15th day of 4th month after fiscal year-end. File via SURI with supporting schedules and CPA-audited financials (if Act 60 Ch. 3 decree holder) (Kreston PR; Sabalier Law).

Apr 15

PR Pass-Through Entity Return (Form 480.20(EC)) — LLCs taxed as partnerships and S-corps file 2025 informational returns. Issue Forms 480.60 EC to partners/shareholders.

Apr 15

Q1 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Individual) — First quarterly estimated payment for 2026 tax year. File through SURI (Sabalier Law; CFS PR).

Apr 15

Q1 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Corporate) — First corporate quarterly installment for calendar-year entities (Kreston PR).

Apr 15

U.S. Form 1040 — Federal individual return due. Bona fide PR residents get automatic 2-month extension to June 15 (but tax still due Apr. 15). Report worldwide income; exclude PR-sourced income under IRC §933. File Form 8898 if this is the year you established PR residency (IRS; Boulay Group, Jan. 2026).

Apr 15

Form 480.6C (International Payments) — Informative returns for payments to nonresidents or for services from sources outside PR due to Hacienda (Bookkeeping Services, Mar. 2025).

Apr 15

Volume of Business Declaration (Patente Municipal) — Annual declaration of gross business income to your municipality for municipal license tax calculation. Due date may vary by municipality; some set it at Apr. 15 to coincide with income tax (EY, Apr. 2025).

Apr 20

IVU Monthly Return — March 2026 IVU due via SURI.

May 2026

May 15

Personal Property Tax Return (Form AS-29i) — File 2025 return with CRIM and pay balance due. If gross receipts exceed $3M, must include CPA-audited financial statements. 3-month extension available (to Aug. 15) but does not extend payment deadline (Kreston PR; CPA Puerto Rico).

May 15

Personal Property Estimated Tax (Q4) — Fourth and final installment for 2025 personal property estimated tax. If paid all four installments on time, 5% discount applies (CPA Puerto Rico).

May 15

Act 20/22 Annual Reports (Prior Law Decrees) — Decree holders under the old Act 20 or Act 22 must file their 2025 annual reports by May 15, 2026, or within 30 days of filing their PR income tax return (including extensions) (DDEC, 2025).

May 20

IVU Monthly Return — April 2026 IVU due via SURI.

June 2026

Jun 15

Q2 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Individual) — Second quarterly estimated payment for 2026 via SURI (Sabalier Law; CFS PR).

Jun 15

Q2 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Corporate) — Second corporate quarterly installment (15th of 6th month) (Kreston PR).

Jun 15

U.S. Form 1040 (PR Resident Auto Extension) — Bona fide PR residents who received the automatic 2-month extension file their federal return by this date (IRS; Boulay Group).

Jun 20

IVU Monthly Return — May 2026 IVU due via SURI.

Month-by-Month Calendar: July – December 2026

July 2026

Jul 20

IVU Monthly Return — June 2026 IVU due via SURI.

Jul 15

LLC Annual Report Reminder — Verify whether your LLC's annual report to the PR Department of State is due. Domestic LLCs pay a $150 annual fee. Missing this filing can lead to administrative dissolution (Relocate PR, Feb. 2024).

August 2026

Aug 15

Personal Property Estimated Tax (Q1 for TY 2026) — First quarterly installment for 2026 personal property tax. Pay through CRIM portal (Kreston PR; CPA Puerto Rico).

Aug 15

Extended Personal Property Tax Return — If you filed a 3-month extension in May, your 2025 Form AS-29i is due today. Tax payment was still due May 15.

Aug 20

IVU Monthly Return — July 2026 IVU due via SURI.

September 2026

Sep 1

Begin Preparing DDEC Annual Report — Not a Hacienda deadline, but set this as your internal start date. You need to gather proof of residency, tax payment evidence, donation receipts, and CPA-audited financials (if export services). The Nov. 15 deadline is firm — DDEC is now auto-fining late filers (Act 60 Investor, Jul. 2025; Tax Notes, Oct. 2025).

Sep 15

Q3 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Individual) — Third quarterly estimated payment for 2026 via SURI.

Sep 15

Q3 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Corporate) — Third corporate quarterly installment (15th of 9th month) (Kreston PR).

Sep 20

IVU Monthly Return — August 2026 IVU due via SURI.

October 2026

Oct 15

Extended PR Individual Income Tax Return — If you filed for a 6-month extension in April, your 2025 Form 482 is due today. File via SURI (LegalClarity; CFS PR).

Oct 15

Extended U.S. Form 1040 — If you filed Form 4868 for a 6-month extension, your federal return is due today (IRS; Boulay Group).

Oct 20

IVU Monthly Return — September 2026 IVU due via SURI.

November 2026 — DDEC Critical Month

Nov 15

DDEC Annual Exempt Business Report (IANE) — This is the most consequential Act 60 deadline. File your 2025 annual report through DDEC's Portal of Incentives (incentives.ddec.pr.gov). Include: proof of residency, tax payment evidence, $10,000 charitable donation receipts, real property ownership documentation, and CPA-audited financial statements (export services). Pay the $5,005 compliance fee with filing. DDEC is now auto-imposing $1,000+ fines for late or missing filings, with escalation to decree revocation (DDEC, 2025; Tax Notes, Oct. 2025; Bookkeeping Services, Jul. 2025).

Nov 15

Personal Property Estimated Tax (Q2 for TY 2026) — Second quarterly installment for 2026 personal property tax via CRIM portal.

Nov 20

IVU Monthly Return — October 2026 IVU due via SURI.

December 2026 — Year-End Compliance

Dec 15

Q4 2026 Estimated Income Tax (Corporate) — Fourth and final corporate estimated installment for calendar-year entities (15th of 12th month) (Kreston PR).

Dec 20

IVU Monthly Return — November 2026 IVU due via SURI.

Dec 31

Complete $10,000 Charitable Donation (Act 60 Ch. 2) — If you haven't yet made your required annual donation ($5,000 to CECFL-approved charity + $5,000 to any PR 501(c)(3) equivalent), complete it by Dec. 31. Obtain official receipts for your DDEC annual report. Recommended: complete by early November to avoid year-end processing delays (WoodsLux, Oct. 2025; PR Advantage; Act 60-2019, §2021.01(b)(4)).

Dec 31

183-Day Presence Test — Ensure you have spent at least 183 days in PR during calendar year 2026. This is the minimum for the bona fide residency presence test under IRC §937. Review your travel log before year-end; if short, adjust travel plans (IRC §937; IRS Pub. 570).

The Bottom Line

Puerto Rico's tax filing landscape is more complex than most mainland states because you are filing with multiple agencies — Hacienda for income tax and IVU, CRIM for property tax, DDEC for Act 60 compliance, the Department of State for corporate maintenance, and the IRS for your federal return. Each has its own portal, its own deadlines, and its own penalty regime.

The four deadlines that most commonly catch Act 60 decree holders off guard are the IVU monthly returns (due the 20th of every month — miss even one and penalties begin), the 480 Series informative returns in February (Hacienda does not grant extensions), the DDEC annual report on November 15 (DDEC is now auto-fining), and the $10,000 charitable donation that must be completed by December 31.

The best defense is a qualified PR-based CPA who manages all these filings across jurisdictions. Set calendar reminders at least 30 days before each major deadline, and maintain a compliance folder with receipts, donation records, presence logs, and brokerage statements year-round. The cost of compliance is a fraction of the cost of losing your decree.
